Product & Service Hub
This is your "Command Center" for everything you sell. It should allow for seamless switching between physical products and intangible services/destinations.
* Unified Inventory List: A searchable table with filters for status (Active, Hidden, Out of Stock, Pending Review).
* The "Regulation" Toggle: A quick-action switch to instantly hide or "delist" a product or destination if it violates terms or becomes unavailable.
* Dynamic Metadata: Fields for descriptions, high-res images, and SEO tags to ensure they look good on the frontend.

2. Revenue & Pricing Engine
This is the most sensitive part of your dashboard. You need a mix of manual overrides and automated logic.
Dynamic Pricing Rules
Instead of changing prices one by one, use a Rule Builder:
* Demand-Based: "Increase price by 15% if destination bookings exceed 80% capacity."
* Time-Based: "Apply 'Early Bird' 10% discount for bookings made 60 days in advance."
* Competitor-Linked: (If integrated) "Match the lowest price on the market within a 5% margin."
Coupon & Offer Management
Feature	Capability
Coupon Architect	Create codes (e.g., SAVE20) with expiration dates and usage limits.
Stacking Logic	Toggle whether a coupon can be used on top of an existing dynamic price.
Flash Sales	A "Big Red Button" to apply a sitewide discount for a specific window (e.g., 4 hours).
3. Destination & Service Regulation
Since destinations often involve third-party vendors or regional logistics, this section requires specific "Safety & Quality" controls.
* Visibility Control: A dedicated "Global Map" view where you can click a region to hide all associated services—useful for sudden weather events or political instability.
* Service Tiers: Categorize destinations (e.g., Elite, Standard, Budget) and apply pricing rules to the entire tier simultaneously.
* Audit Logs: Track who changed a destination status and why, ensuring accountability in the admin team.

4. Customer Behavior & Analytics
To regulate effectively, you need to know how users are interacting with your changes.
* The "Pathfinder" View: Visualizing the user journey—where do they drop off? (e.g., "Users are clicking 'Paris' but leaving once they see the Dynamic Price.")
* Heatmaps & Interaction: Integration with tools like Hotjar or Microsoft Clarity directly in the dashboard to see which destination photos get the most clicks.
* Conversion Monitoring: Real-time data on how a new coupon or price change affects the "Add to Cart" rate.
Pro Tip: Implement "Role-Based Access Control" (RBAC). Your marketing intern should be able to add coupons, but only a Senior Admin should have the power to delete a destination or change the core dynamic pricing algorithms.

Suggested Dashboard Layout
* Sidebar: Dashboard Home, Product Manager, Destination Hub, Pricing Rules, Coupons, Analytics, User Logs.
* Top Bar: Global Search (find a product/user/order instantly) and System Status alerts.
* Main Canvas: Modular widgets that can be rearranged based on what you are focusing on that day.
